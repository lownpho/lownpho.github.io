Risks of licking eyeballs

April 7, 2025

Licking eyeballs is a bizarre and potentially dangerous practice that has gained some attention in recent years. While it may seem like a harmless act, there are several risks associated with licking eyeballs that should be considered before engaging in this unusual behavior.

One of the primary risks of licking eyeballs is the potential for transmitting bacteria and viruses. The human mouth is home to a variety of microorganisms, some of which can cause infections if they come into contact with the delicate tissues of the eye. This can lead to conditions such as conjunctivitis, keratitis, or even more serious eye infections.

Additionally, licking eyeballs can cause physical damage to the eye. The tongue is not a sterile instrument, and the act of licking can introduce foreign particles or irritants into the eye, leading to discomfort or injury. Furthermore, the moisture from saliva can disrupt the natural tear film, causing dryness and irritation.

Another concern is the potential for allergic reactions. Some individuals may be allergic to certain proteins or substances found in saliva, which can trigger an allergic response when they come into contact with the eye. This can result in symptoms such as redness, swelling, and itching.

Story time

In 2013, a young man from Cork died due to complications arising from his girlfriend licking his eyeball, a practice that led to a severe bacterial infection. The infection spread rapidly, causing permanent damage to his cornea and eventually leading to sepsis. This tragic incident serves as a stark reminder of the serious consequences that can result from engaging in such risky behavior. Medical professionals strongly advise against any contact between the mouth and eyes to prevent similar tragedies.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while licking eyeballs may seem like a quirky or humorous act, it is important to recognize the potential risks involved. From bacterial infections to physical damage and allergic reactions, the dangers associated with this practice far outweigh any perceived benefits. It is advisable to avoid licking eyeballs altogether and to prioritize eye health and safety.
